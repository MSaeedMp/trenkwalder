Code of Conduct

Our company is committed to providing a respectful and inclusive workplace for everyone. All employees, contractors, and visitors are expected to follow these guidelines. This code applies to all work-related activities, including those outside regular office hours and off-site events.

Respect and Professionalism

Treat all colleagues with dignity and respect. Discrimination, harassment, or bullying of any kind is strictly prohibited. This includes but is not limited to behavior based on race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, or national origin. We are committed to equal opportunity in all aspects of employment, from hiring to promotions to daily interactions.

If you experience or witness disrespectful behavior, speak up. You can address it directly with the person involved, raise it with your manager, or use the formal reporting channels described below. Bystander silence allows harmful behavior to continue.

Communication

Use professional and courteous language in all workplace communications, including email, chat, and meetings. Listen actively and give others the opportunity to share their perspectives. Avoid interrupting, dismissing, or talking over colleagues.

Written communication should be clear and considerate. Remember that tone can be difficult to convey in text. When in doubt, opt for a video call or in-person conversation. Keep meeting invites focused and include agendas so participants can prepare.

Confidentiality

Protect company and client information at all times. Do not share confidential data with unauthorized parties. This includes financial data, client lists, strategic plans, employee personal information, and proprietary technology.

Use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication on all company accounts, and lock your workstation when leaving your desk. Do not use personal email or messaging apps for company business. Report any suspected data breaches to the IT security team immediately at security@trenkwalder.com.

Conflicts of Interest

Avoid situations where personal interests may conflict with the company's interests. Disclose any potential conflicts to your manager or the compliance team. This includes outside employment, financial interests in competitors or clients, and personal relationships that could influence business decisions.

Accept gifts from business partners only if they are of nominal value (under €50). Gifts above this threshold must be reported to compliance. Invitations to events or trips funded by business partners require prior approval.

Use of Company Resources

Company equipment, software, and facilities are provided for business use. Limited personal use is acceptable, but should not interfere with work responsibilities or violate any company policies. Do not install unauthorized software on company devices.

Company credit cards and expense accounts must be used strictly for business purposes. Personal expenses charged to the company will be treated as a policy violation.

Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation of this code, report it through the designated channels. You can contact your direct manager, HR, or the anonymous ethics hotline at ethics@trenkwalder.com. Reports can be made anonymously through the online portal.

The company takes all reports seriously and will investigate promptly. Retaliation against anyone who reports a violation in good faith is strictly prohibited and will itself be treated as a violation of this code.

Consequences

Violations of this code may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. The severity of the action will depend on the nature, frequency, and circumstances of the violation. Minor first-time offenses may result in a verbal warning, while serious or repeated violations will lead to written warnings or termination.
